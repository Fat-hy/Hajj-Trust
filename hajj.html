Hajj Journey - Step by Step

Before 8th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 1: Ihram (Intention and Attire)

Ihram is the first step in Hajj, where pilgrims enter a state of purity by wearing specific attire and making the intention (niyyah) to perform Hajj. Pilgrims must be in a state of Ihram when they reach the designated boundary (Miqat), just outside Mecca.

Spiritual significance: This state represents the pilgrim’s commitment to the Hajj journey and marks their detachment from worldly distractions.

8th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 2: Tawaf (Circumambulation of the Kaaba)

Tawaf involves walking seven times around the Kaaba in a counterclockwise direction. Pilgrims perform this ritual at the Grand Mosque upon arrival. It is an act of submission and devotion to Allah, with each round symbolizing spiritual renewal.

Spiritual significance: Tawaf represents the unity of the believer with the will of Allah and the continuous flow of worship towards Him.

"And when We made the House a place of return for mankind and a place of security." (Quran 2:125)

8th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 3: Sa'i (Walking between Safa and Marwa)

Sa'i is the act of walking seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwa, located within the Grand Mosque. It symbolizes Hajar’s search for water for her son, Isma'il. Pilgrims perform this act after Tawaf.

Spiritual significance: Sa'i commemorates the faith and perseverance of Hajar, emphasizing the importance of patience and reliance on Allah during hardships.

9th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 4: Wuquf (Standing on Arafat)

Wuquf is one of the most important parts of Hajj, where pilgrims gather at the plain of Arafat to stand in prayer and supplication. This is the day of forgiveness and mercy, and pilgrims seek Allah’s grace and mercy during their time on Arafat.

Spiritual significance: Wuquf symbolizes the day of judgment when all of humanity will stand before Allah. It’s a day to repent and pray for one’s sins to be forgiven.

"Indeed, Allah frees more people from the Fire on the Day of Arafah than on any other day." (Sahih Muslim)

Night of 9th to 10th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 5: Muzdalifah

Muzdalifah is the location where pilgrims spend the night after leaving Arafat. Pilgrims perform prayers and collect pebbles for the next ritual, stoning the devil (Rami al-Jamarat).

Spiritual significance: Muzdalifah is a time for reflection, prayer, and peaceful rest. Pilgrims stay in an open area, free from distractions, as they prepare for the next stage of the Hajj journey.

10th, 11th, and 12th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 6: Rami al-Jamarat (Stoning the Devil)

Rami al-Jamarat is the act of throwing pebbles at three stone pillars in Mina, symbolizing the rejection of evil. Pilgrims perform this ritual as a reminder of Prophet Ibrahim’s rejection of Satan's temptation.

Spiritual significance: Stoning the devil signifies the believer’s victory over temptation and desire, and a reminder to remain firm in faith.

10th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 7: Sacrifice

Sacrifice (Qurbani) is a ritual performed on the 10th of Dhu al-Hijjah to commemorate Prophet Ibrahim’s willingness to sacrifice his son, Isma'il. Pilgrims may sacrifice an animal, which is then distributed among the poor, friends, and family.

Spiritual significance: The sacrifice symbolizes submission to Allah’s will and the believer’s willingness to sacrifice for the sake of Allah.

11th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 8: Tawaf al-Ifadah

Tawaf al-Ifadah is a compulsory circumambulation of the Kaaba performed after the sacrifice. It symbolizes the pilgrim’s continued devotion to Allah.

10th of Dhu al-Hijjah

By TMHT Team

Step 9: Shaving or Trimming Hair

After completing Tawaf al-Ifadah, male pilgrims typically shave their heads, while females trim a small portion of their hair. This act represents spiritual renewal and the shedding of past sins.

Last day of Hajj

By TMHT Team

Step 10: Tawaf al-Wada

Tawaf al-Wada is the final Tawaf, performed before leaving Mecca. It is an emotional and reflective act, as pilgrims bid farewell to the Kaaba and return home.
